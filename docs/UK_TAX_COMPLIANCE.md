## âš ï¸ HMRC Allowable Expenses Rules

### "Wholly and Exclusively" Test
For an expense to be allowable, it must be:
1. **Wholly** = entirely for business purposes
2. **Exclusively** = not for personal use

### ALLOWED âœ…
- Business premises rent
- Business equipment
- Stock/raw materials
- Business travel (not commuting)
- Business phone/internet (proportion)
- Professional subscriptions
- Business insurance
- Accountancy fees
- Business banking fees
- Advertising/marketing
- Staff costs
- Training related to business

### NOT ALLOWED âŒ
- Personal expenses
- Commuting to regular workplace
- Entertainment (meals, drinks with clients)*
- Personal clothing (even if "business attire")
- Fines and penalties
- Personal subscriptions
- Gym membership
- Personal phone/internet proportion
- Client gifts over Â£50 per person per year

*Entertainment expenses are generally NOT allowable unless overnight business travel

### PARTIALLY ALLOWED âš¡
- **Home office:** Can claim proportion of:
  - Rent/mortgage interest (business % only)
  - Council tax
  - Utilities
  - Internet
  - Home insurance

- **Vehicle:** Either:
  - Simplified: 45p/mile (first 10,000 miles), 25p/mile (after)
  - Actual costs: Fuel, insurance, repairs (business % only)

## âš ï¸ Self-Employment vs Employment

### Self-Employment (SA103):
- **You are self-employed if:**
  - You work for yourself
  - You take on risk and responsibility
  - You can send substitutes
  - You control how/when work is done
  - You provide own equipment
  - You invoice for services

### Employment (SA102):
- **You are employed if:**
  - Employer controls your work
  - Fixed hours/location
  - Employer provides equipment
  - Paid via PAYE
  - Holiday pay, sick pay
  - Notice period

**The Road Centre Example:**
- If Jemma invoices them = Self-employed âœ“
- If they pay her PAYE = Employed âœ“
- Check her payslips/invoices to confirm
